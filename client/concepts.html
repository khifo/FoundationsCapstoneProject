Football Concepts

Pass Game

Here are some common passing concepts at all levels

    The mesh concept is crossing routes over the middle. It is great against man coverage.
    The smash concept is a corner route and a hitch route. It is greate against zone coverage.
    The verticals concept uses vertical routes to overload deep zones.
    Double slant is where two receivers are running a slant route on the same side. It is great against Cover 2 and man coverage.

Run Game

Here are some common run concepts at all levels.

    Power is a gap scheme run where a backside linemen pulls to wrap through the hole. A fullback or tight end would kick out.
    Counter is a gap scheme run where two linemen pull. It is similar to power.
    Trap is a quick hitting play that intentionally leaves an inside defensive linemen unblocked.
    Toss is a an outside run play where as many as three players can pull depending on the design.

Coverages on Defense

Football coverage is how a defense defends the pass. With the popularity of spread offense at all levels, it is important to know the coverages and how to scheme against it. The number in coverage indicates how many players defend deep.

    Cover 0 has no player deep, usually tied with a heavy blitz package, and utilizes man coverage.
    Cover 1 has 1 player deep in middle of the field with man coverage underneath.
    Cover 2 has 2 players deep each covering one half of the field with either zone or man coverage underneath.
    Cover 3 has 3 players deep with each player covering 1/3 of the field.
    Cover 4 has 4 players deep with each player covering 1/4 of the field. Also known as quarters coverage.
